SSA form is an alternative IR where each variable is assigned exactly once. Every original mutable update is turned into a new binding, and φ-functions merge values coming from different control-flow paths. Notably, SSA’s model of computation is essentially “binding-oriented” like functional languages: variables in SSA are never mutated; each assignment yields a new binding

. In fact, SSA is formally equivalent to a subset of CPS (ignoring exceptional control flow)

. In both CPS and SSA each value has a single definition and uses refer to a particular definition. The difference is that CPS enforces this lexically (via lexical α-renaming), whereas SSA enforces it via dominance in the control-flow graph
